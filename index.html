Bradley Simpson

Singer

Bradley William Simpson (July 28, 1995) is apart of the British band, The Vamps, along with Connor Ball, James McVey and Tristan Evans. Simpson met fellow band member James McVey via YouTube in 2011, together they formed the band The Vamps with## Tristan Evans and Connor Ball.

Connor Ball

Bassist

Connor Samuel John Ball (né Stephen, born March 15, 1996) is a part of the British band, The Vamps, along with Bradley Simpson, James McVey, and Tristan Evans. He is the bassist of the band. Connor was originally putting up covers on YouTube, just like fellow band members Bradley Simpson and James McVey. He was the last member to join the group. He is the only Scottish member.

Bradley Simpson, James McVey, and Tristan Evans met Connor Ball via a mutual friend and soon formed the band, The Vamps

Tristan Evans

Drummer

Tristan Oliver Vance Evans (August 15, 1994) is apart of the British band, The Vamps, along with Bradley Simpson, Connor Ball and James McVey. He is the drummer and backing vocalist in the band. He also acts as a producer for the band, particularly with their covers or "Tristan E" drum versions of songs.

In 2012, Bradley Simpson and James McVey met Tristan Evans through Facebook. The trio then met Connor Ball via a mutual friend and soon formed the band, The Vamps.

About me

The Vamps

Their debut single "Can We Dance" was released in 2013 and achieved significant chart success. This was followed by their debut album, "Meet the Vamps," in 2014. Over the years, The Vamps have released multiple albums, including "Wake Up" (2015), "Night & Day" (2017), and "Cherry Blossom" (2020). Known for their catchy melodies and energetic performances, The Vamps have toured extensively, both as headliners and as support acts for larger artists.

The band's style combines elements of pop, rock, and synth-pop, and they are recognized for their vibrant, youthful image and engaging live shows.

Have gone from strength to strength over the years since releasing their debut album 'Meet the Vamp's in 2014. With huge hits under their belt including 'All Night', 'Somebody To You' and 'Last Night', the boys have amassed a legion of loyal fans. To help you get to know The Vamps a little better, we've taken a look at everything you need to know about the band.Their birthdays are above, which makes Brad currently 28-years-old, James 30, Tristan 29 and Connor 28-years-old.

In an interview with disney.com, Bradley revealed that he and James came up with the idea for the band name when they were just going through a list of potential names. When they came to The Vamps he said it just 'felt right.Connor added: "It also means a throwing together of things, which I guess we like to think we did as a band!"

In another interview, with Hollywood Life, the band revealed they were big fans of The Vampire Diaries and according to James: "Tristan’s a massive fan of Twilight".The Vamps first formed in 2011. James McVey, who already had management, decided he'd like to be part of a band, and subsequently found Brad Simpson through his YouTube channel. The pair wrote songs together in late 2011, and they met drummer Tristan Evans through Facebook in 2012. The three of them then met Connor Ball through a mutual friend.

They uploaded cover songs to their YouTube channel in the summer of 2012, with their performance of One Direction's 'Live While We're Young' being a particular fan-favourite. From here, they grew a huge fanbase who were extremely supportive of the band.
